3D Gaussian Deb-latting:
Gaussian Splatting for Motion Blur Images

Jeongtaek Oh, Jaeyoung Chung, Dongwoo Lee , Kyoung Mu Lee

Computer Vision Lab (Seoul National University), NAVER AI Center  

Paper (Not yet) Code (Releasing Soon)

Abstract

We propose 3D Gaussian Deb-latting: a method to optimize sharp 3D Gaussian Splatting with blurry set of images. 3D Gaussian Splatting has risen as an emerging trend in both of computer vision and graphics fields for its high fidelity and also rendering speed. However, it is challenging to obtain high-quality sharp scene when we have only blurry images caused by extreme motion. To address this, we incorporate auxiliary curve estimation model which optimizes camera trajectory explaining motion for each input images. To be specific, our pipeline optimizes Bezier curve representing camera motion, where we sample multiple camera poses whose average of rendering is supervised with blurry observations. Our model achieves state-of-the-art performance in terms of qualtiy and efficiency in not only synthetic dataset made from Blender, but also real datasets (Real-Camera-Motion-Blur and Exblur.)

Method

We jointly optimize gaussian splatting with camera motion trajectory which explains blurry observation when averaged.
